# HR Analytics Report

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes employee data to understand workforce trends, employee attrition, job satisfaction, compensation patterns, and factors influencing employee retention.

The dataset contains information for 1,470 employees across multiple departments and job roles.

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| Metric                 | Value    |
| ---------------------- | -------- |
| Total Employees        | 1470     |
| Attrition Rate         | 16.12%   |
| Average Monthly Income | $6502.93 |

### Analysis Findings

### 1. Employee Attrition

The overall attrition rate is 16.12%, indicating that approximately one out of every six employees has left the organization.

High attrition can increase recruitment costs, training expenses, and productivity loss.

### 2. Department-wise Analysis

Employee attrition varies across departments, suggesting that certain departments experience greater retention challenges.

Departments with higher attrition should be investigated further to identify workload, management, or engagement-related concerns.

### 3. Overtime Impact

The analysis indicates a relationship between overtime work and employee attrition.

Employees working overtime appear more likely to leave the organization compared to employees who do not work overtime regularly.

This suggests workload management may be an important factor in employee retention.

### 4. Job Satisfaction Analysis

Job satisfaction levels show a clear relationship with employee retention.

Employees reporting lower satisfaction levels are more likely to leave the company.

Improving employee engagement programs may help reduce attrition.

### 5. Income Analysis

Monthly income distribution reveals variation in employee compensation.

Compensation can play a significant role in employee retention and should be reviewed periodically to remain competitive.

### 6. Age Distribution

Employee age distribution indicates that attrition is more common within specific age groups.

Career growth opportunities and targeted retention strategies may help improve employee stability.

## Business Recommendations

### Recommendation 1

Monitor employees working excessive overtime and implement workload balancing initiatives.

### Recommendation 2

Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys to identify engagement concerns early.

### Recommendation 3

Review compensation structures for employee groups showing higher attrition rates.

### Recommendation 4

Develop retention programs focused on departments experiencing elevated employee turnover.

### Recommendation 5

Provide career development and growth opportunities to improve employee engagement and long-term retention.

## Conclusion

The analysis highlights employee attrition as a significant workforce challenge. Overtime, job satisfaction, compensation, and department-level factors all appear to influence employee retention.

By implementing data-driven workforce strategies, organizations can improve employee satisfaction, reduce turnover, and enhance overall business performance.
